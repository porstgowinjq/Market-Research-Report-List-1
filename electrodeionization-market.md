<p><ul><li>Plate and Frame Construction</li><li>Spiral Wound Construction</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Electrodeionization (EDI) market features two primary construction types: Plate and Frame, and Spiral Wound. Plate and Frame construction consists of stacked flat plates, allowing for efficient ion removal while facilitating easy maintenance and scalability. In contrast, Spiral Wound construction involves winding membrane sheets around a central tube, maximizing surface area in a compact design, which enhances ion exchange efficiency. Both types cater to applications requiring high purity water, but their design impacts operational efficiency and space requirements.</p></p>
